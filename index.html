Dublin Theatre Festival 2018 drew to a close on Sunday 14 October. For 18 days and nights, the festival presented a selection of brand new work, fresh versions of classic plays, adventurous international productions created by renowned artists as well as emerging voices, a series of critical talks and artist development programmes.

Performances and Events

Bluebeard's Castle | 12-14 Oct
Writer/director Enda Walsh and the award-winning team that brought you The Second Violinist in a new production of Béla Bartók’s searing, early 20th- century reimagining of the tale of Bluebeard.
A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man | 26 Sept - 7 Oct
From Rough Magic comes the world premiere of Arthur Riordan’s vibrant stage adaptation of the seminal novel by James Joyce, A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man, brought to life by Ronan Phelan.
The Mai | 25-29 Sept
From Decadent Theatre a major revival of Marina Carr’s award winning breakthrough play The Mai, an epic tale of three generations of a family inspired by love but shattered by inescapable reality featuring a stellar cast led by Derbhle Crotty.

Wheteher this is your first time seeing a festival show or you have been coming for years, there's something for you. There's a selection of brand new work, fresh versions of classic plays and adventurous international productions, created by renowned artists as well as emerging voices, and aimed at children, young people and adult;

The programm showcases many different ways that artists use theatre to engage with the world they're living in. It's a world where individuals, communities and the environment are under threat but also one where the desire for human connection is strong and the radical possibilities of imagination burn fiercely against the advancing darkness.

Venues

Gaiety Theatre
Established in 1871, The Gaiety Theatre is situated on South King Street, just a moment from Stephen’s Green. For 144 years, the theatre has presented the highest quality musical and dramatic entertainment.
Samuel Beckett Theatre
The Samuel Beckett Theatre was opened in 1992 to celebrate the quarter-centenary of Trinity College Dublin. It is the campus theatre of the University's Department of Drama and hosts some of the most prestigious companies from Ireland and abroad.
Bord Gáis Energy Theatre
This superb 2,100 capacity purpose built theatre was designed by world renowned architect Daniel Libeskind and is an outstanding addition to Ireland’s Theatre world.

Festival productions take diverse forms, form intimate encounters in found spaces to the grand physical and emotional scale of Shakespearena drama, ballet and opera. There are portraits of individuals corrupted by power, testimonies from dissenting voices and challenges to received narratives. Some plays teem with the words and others are stripped back to essentials. What connects each is the need for an audience to gather togehter in a shared time and space. At a live performance we are alone with our thoughts but also participants in the public act that makes theatre happen.
